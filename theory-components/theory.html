Critical Reflection Essay – Exam Commercial Website

Introducing The Aim of Essay

In this essay, I will explore the implications of website development, focusing on its connection to the global economy and its influence on art and design techniques. I will reflect on how these concepts have shaped my approach and the ways I have applied them in my work. I will begin by discussing the design process of my exam project, highlighting the new strategies I employed to create user-friendly UI/UX designs. This includes an examination of the interactivity features I implemented and the narrative elements that support the project’s overall theme. Next, I will address the challenges I encountered during the development phase. I will detail how I navigated these obstacles and transformed them into learning opportunities, ultimately strengthening my design objectives. Finally, I will reflect on my personal growth throughout this project. I will consider how the skills and insights I’ve gained will support my future career as a web designer and align with professional practices in the industry. With that in mind, let us dive into this critical reflection essay.

The Global Economic and Artistic Implications of Web Technologies

The Cambridge Dictionary defines the global economy as “the way in which economies have been developing to operate together as one system” (Cambridge Dictionary). Understanding this concept is crucial for knowing how it connects to website development. By exploring multiple definitions, I found that the Cambridge Dictionary’s explanation stood out the most with me. It highlights that the global economy refers to the connection that occurs between multiple countries who work within economic activities that will create a crucial positive benefit on the countries involved. Through this definition I had to find out how the global economy is connected with the website development, which becomes clear when we consider the economic advantages that websites can bring. These include market expansion, which looks at how businesses needs to ubiquity skills to enable them to connect with audiences internationally without having to physically travel. Through this process, business are able to reach target audiences and sales through websites. The second advantage includes the growth of e-commerce, which looks at aspects of online communication and online platforms. This has become the most efficient new way of being able to connect with clients, offering them services and product online. This has boosted global trade and economic activity as it offers immersive diverse opportunities and job creation in the industry of website development. Lasty the job creation, the new digital space made things easy and efficient in the creative industry. There is a high demand in the world of digital marketing, new digital skills and techniques are always brought up contributing to creating new job opportunities and economic growth.

How Global Economy Impacts Artistic Design

Global economy has made an impact in arts and design in spaces of website development. As a website designer it is crucial to understand, acknowledge and research the art style and design techniques you are going to employ in the project. Website designers needs to look beyond beauty and arts and understand culture of the economy they are working with, respecting and understanding the diversity by looking at the factors of being able to employ cultural aesthetics, themes and content that resonate with diverse clients or company. There should be a background story through each implementation of design, understand colors used, pictures, friendly UI/UX design and user experience. Beyond this, website designers needs to understand the type of content they are going to provide or what the client has requested, since this includes global interconnection, it is important to understand the language and cultural content. Understanding this content, made me look broader at the implications that I have fundamentally used in my design project, how I have incorporated design techniques that carry cultural content, narrative story, the client and his preferred aesthetics and also how I have to develop and smooth functional user engagement that will enhance my project usability and development.

The Design Techniques I Have Used.

Through understanding these techniques, I have implemented the design techniques that aligned with the author I am presenting on my website named Stephen King. Stephen King is a best seller award winning author who was born in Portland, Maine, in 1947. He is mostly known for publishing books and novels that resonate with the following genres, horror, suspense and fantasy. I had to consider his cultural and aesthetic style that he usually resonate with, through look at the horror genre, I had to choose the color scheme wisely and see his other previous website that he has. Mosty of his work combine and dwell on dark colors, however I had to will modify them, to make interactivity easier and creating friendly user engagement through complying 60-30-10 rule. Stephen King works lean and evoke around dark colors which evoke emotions of dark and suspenseful atmospheres as he mostly writes horror books. His colors are leaning towards darker, moody colors like deep blacks, grays, and muted tones. However, the colors that I picked where the following: White smoke, charcoal blue and smoke red.

How My UI/UX designs, data visualisations, created meaning and successfully uses friendly user engagement.

UI/UX Designs

I had to reflect on my previous website design and analyze the feedback I received. By reviewing this feedback, I refined my project by changing the user experience design and implementing a new approach that aligns with the client’s aesthetic, as their work mostly focuses on horror, suspense, and fantasy. I conducted brief research to understand how authors’ websites are typically structured and to determine how I could make my design both neat and efficient. I had to provide several pages that will include the content behind my website and academic requirements I need to implement on my website. To enhance user-friendly engagement, I highlighted important buttons with an attractive, soft red color, using it sparingly (about 10% of the website) to draw attention to key content. Additionally, since the website contains several pages, I implemented a dropdown menu to organize them neatly, ensuring readability without overwhelming the website or its users. Lastly, I incorporated animation styles that seamlessly blend with the website’s theme and enhance its interactivity.

Data Visualization

With data visualization, I had to reflect on the mistakes I previously made and deepen my understanding of what effective data visualization entails. This includes exploring how users interact with visual data, especially when it’s interactive, and how it presents a narrative to help users understand the content of the data that has been displayed. For this project, I used a complex API focused on Stephen King’s books. This API provided extensive data, including information about his works, achievements such as nominations and awards, the number of pages per book, publication years, and even the villains featured in each story. My challenge was to decide which data to present in my interactive visualization and how I want to provide a better understanding analysis and communication of information I will display. I opted to create graphical representations of the data using bar graphs and pie charts. For instance, I used a bar graph to plot the publication years on the x-axis and compare the number of pages for each book on y-axis. This approach this approach, I have created meaning successfully. Additionally, I reconsidered my design techniques, particularly the color scheme. I replaced the previous colors with a palette that better aligns with both the author’s dark and suspenseful aesthetic and the overall theme of my website. To enhance user engagement, I included interactive buttons that allow users to toggle certain data points on or off, providing them with a more customizable experience.

The Role of Interactivity in Your Project and Its Impact on User Experience and Data Interpretation

The role of interactivity in this project was to provide users with an engaging and user-friendly experience while exploring my website. Interactivity is present on every page, starting with the navigation bar and dropdown menus, which allow users to seamlessly explore different sections and their respective content. The navigation bar offers an immersive experience, enabling users to switch between pages from one page to another. This allows users to engage with the whole website effectively and friendly. I also incorporated various interactive elements, such as buttons, each designed to produce a specific outcome. For instance, the "Read More" button on the About page allows users to delve deeper into the author’s biography, sourced from Wikipedia. This feature gives users an opportunity to learn more about the author, including his career, achievements, and personal milestones. Additionally, users can explore related images via a bottom-scroller bar. To further enhance the user experience, I provided a link to the full Wikipedia page, enabling users to dive deeper into the author’s life and works. Other interesting interactive features include social media icons in the footer, which direct users to Stephen King’s personal websites and fan pages, keeping them updated with his latest news and activities. These interactive techniques significantly improve the user experience, making the website more engaging, accessible, and informative.

The Most Pressing Challenges Faced During Development and How They Were Tackled

Throughout the design process, I faced several challenges that ultimately helped me grow, think critically, and refine my skills as a website developer. Each obstacle pushed me to find creative solutions and improve my design approach. The first major challenge was determining the right UI/UX design to implement. I was working with a client who is an author specializing in horror, suspense, and fantasy novels. His aesthetic needed to be reflected on the website, and I also had to consider his demographic as an adult male. Choosing the appropriate design, art style, and other techniques was difficult because I wanted all elements to harmonize and align with the project’s theme. To overcome this, I critically analyzed the feedback from my lecturers and used it to develop a more engaging and visually appealing design. I conducted research on how website are structured, the type of content they provide and how users engage with it. This research informed the redesign of my wireframes and style guide. Another significant challenge was selecting a color scheme that accurately represented the author’s brand. I explored his book covers, original website, and various online discourses about his style, taste, themes, and aesthetics. I identified the colors he frequently uses in his work and incorporated them into my design to ensure consistency and relevance. These challenges helped me gain a deeper understanding of the web development process, think like a designer, and develop problem-solving strategies. Each challenge strengthened my ability to create meaningful and effective design solutions.

How Your Project Aligns with Professional Practices in International Web/App Development

That’s an interesting question. I’m not entirely sure if I can answer it definitively, as I only began my journey as a web developer a few months ago, and this is the second website I’ve created. However, I’ve learned a great deal throughout this experience. I’ve realized that web development is not just about coding and designing, it’s about thinking like a critical designer. It involves understanding why you choose specific design elements, the meaning behind colors, and how to effectively solve challenges. One of the most important lessons I’ve learned is the value of designing with the user in mind. It’s essential to test your website by inviting others to engage with it and gather feedback on its interactivity and user-friendliness. This practice mirrors the iterative testing process used in professional web and app development. Although I’m still at the beginning of my journey, I believe the skills I’ve acquired during this project I have gained the following skills: critical thinking, user-centered design, and problem-solving are foundational in international web development. These skills will be invaluable as I take on larger projects with the potential to reach global audiences. This project also became the first long professional work that I have produced throughout my learning experience, I believe it will part of my portfolio, and I will reflect back to it as I grow stronger in the field of website development
