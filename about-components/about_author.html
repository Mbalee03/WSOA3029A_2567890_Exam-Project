ABOUT AUTHOR

Stephen King, born in Portland, Maine, in 1947, is one of the world’s most famous and prolific authors, known for his mastery of horror, suspense, and fantasy. His debut novel, ‘Carrie’ (1974), launched his career, followed by iconic works like The Shining, It, and The Stand. With over 60 novels and 200 short stories, many adapted into films and TV series. Stephen King's influence on popular culture is immense. He has received numerous awards, including the National Medal of Arts and multiple Bram Stoker Awards. Stephen King currently lives in Maine and Florida with his wife, Tabitha, continuing his legacy as a literary giant. His influence extends far beyond the written word, with his stories shaping the landscape of horror across various media. His works have captivated audiences globally, with adaptations reaching new generations through movies, TV series, and even stage productions. Stephen King's ability to blend relatable human emotions with supernatural elements has not only redefined horror but also expanded its appeal.
